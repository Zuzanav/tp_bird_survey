Torrey Pines Bird Survey

Torrey Pines

Torrey Pines State Natural Reserve is a reserve located in San Diego, California. It is home to the rarest pine tree in North America. The reserve offers a lot of biodiversity including all the different species of birds that may use San Diego as their pitstop along their migratory path, or even call it their permanent home. The Torrey Pines Docent Society conducts bird surveys every month. Seeing as the data is very valuable, I have decided to create this app to help display the data for all to see.


The Data

The data includes up to 172 unique species of birds over the course of 15 years. Because the data spans a large amount of time, it is easy to see the seasonality of some birds. In the graph below, you can select up to 5 different types of birds to compare. You may also search for specific birds if you already have some in mind. Click and drag over any section of the timeline to get a closer look at the numbers for that given time period. Mostly explore, have fun and see if you learn anything!


Bird Trends

While looking at the visualized data I noticed trends in certain birds. For example, it seems like the California Quail population trends downwards over the years, or the California Thrasher trends upwards. Note this trend is localized and may infer a lot about what may be happening at Torrey Pines or San Diego County, even, since the California Thrasher's population is generally declining in California. Interestingly enough there are some birds who also appear to have an inverted bell-curve, indicating their population bounced back after decreasing for a certain amount of years (ex: Royal Tern). In order to increase the visibility of the trends, the data displayed below is a moving average of the data displayed above.

Notice Any Errors?

If you are an experienced birder and notice an error, please contact me. Some of the birds have had their common names change in the past decade and a half and may result in what appears to have half their data missing when in reality, the second half of their data lies in a different common name.
